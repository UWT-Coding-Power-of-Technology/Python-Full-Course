Day 19 Grammar: Passive Voice – Present & Past

Passive voice is used when we focus on the action or object rather than the subject who does it.

1. Structure
Tense	Structure	Example
Present Simple	am/is/are + past participle	The book is read by many students.
Past Simple	was/were + past participle	The cake was eaten by the kids.
2. Quick Tips

The doer (agent) is optional:

✅ The door was opened. (We don’t always need to say who opened it.)

To make a sentence passive:

Active: She writes a letter.

Passive: A letter is written by her.

Questions in passive:

Is the homework done?

Was the project finished on time?

3. Mini Exercise

Turn these active sentences into passive:

The teacher explains the lesson.

The chef cooked a delicious meal.

People speak English all over the world.

They painted the house last week.
